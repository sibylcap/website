the friction

voting power decays linearly across 48 hours.
so does your sleep.

reppo voters lock $reppo into veREPPO and allocate that voting power across datanets. within each 48-hour epoch, voting power decays linearly. early conviction is worth more than late momentum-following. there is no slashing — voter quality is policed by opportunity cost and two-sided voting alone.

which means voting well requires reading every publisher submission across every datanet you hold a position in, applying judgment, and casting stake — fast — every 48 hours. across 9+ live datanets. per epoch.

  • 019+ live datanets as of v2 launch. 700+ submissions per epoch across the network.
  • 02linear decay means a vote at hour 6 is worth ~87% of hour 0. a vote at hour 36 is worth 25%. a vote at hour 47 is worth nothing.
  • 03no slashing. the only cost of bad voting is opportunity cost on locked capital and being shorted by two-sided voters.
  • 04performance pool distributes 5% every third epoch. consistent early conviction compounds.

how it works

three phases. one configuration. zero late-night votes.

01

setup

one-time. minutes, not days.

  • connect wallet via SIWE
  • declare which datanets you hold veREPPO in
  • encode 4–6 quality criteria per datanet
  • set per-epoch voting budget
  • auto-vote or recommend-only
  • optional: upload exemplars for calibration
02

run

every 48-hour epoch. the first 6 hours.

  • watch publisher events
  • read submission contents
  • apply your rubric
  • cross-check publisher history
  • cast votes within budget · or surface for confirm
  • log every decision with rationale
03

reflect

after every performance pool distribution.

  • compare votes vs reward outcomes
  • flag rubric criteria not predictive
  • surface strong-fit datanets · weak-fit datanets
  • recommend veREPPO reallocation
  • tune the rubric for next epoch
  • compound
