01 / GTM Growth Plan

Executive Summary

SIBYL is an autonomous AI advisory agent operating on Base. It evaluates early-stage crypto projects, acquires stakes in the ones worth its attention, and provides structured advisory to founders. It has built its own infrastructure: Ping (on-chain messaging protocol, 30 users), a staking contract with tiered multipliers, 6 paid x402 API endpoints, and an autonomous trading engine (Talos). The $SIBYL token launched on Virtuals Protocol, graduated to Uniswap on March 18, 2026, and has active staking and LP. This is not a launch plan. This is a growth plan for a product that already works.

Core value proposition: SIBYL is the first autonomous agent with a verifiable on-chain track record of evaluating, acquiring, and advising real projects. Not a chatbot. An operator.

Market Context

  • Base ecosystem: L2 with growing agent economy. Virtuals Protocol is the leading agent launchpad. Agent-to-agent commerce emerging via ACP. Low gas enables micro-transactions and frequent on-chain interactions.
  • Competitive landscape: AIXBT (analytics/signal agent), CLANKER (token deployer), various chatbot wrappers. Most agents post. Few build infrastructure. Fewer have advisory track records.
  • SIBYL's position: Differentiated by proof-of-work. Built Ping protocol, staking contract (54 tests), trading engine, x402 payment rails. The on-chain record is the moat. No other agent has shipped this volume of real infrastructure.
  • Gap addressed: Founders on Base have technical skill but lack vision, narrative clarity, and product strategy. SIBYL fills that gap with structured advisory backed by on-chain capital commitment.

Retention Loops by Segment

Power Users (stakers + Ping users + active community)

  • Governance preview: First access to acquisition vote when token governance launches. Power users shape the portfolio.
  • Creator tax sharing: 1% tax generates continuous revenue. Power users with highest staking tiers get proportional share.
  • Advisory access: Power users can submit project leads directly via Ping with priority evaluation.
  • Field report early access: Weekly field reports shared in Discord 24h before X post.

Regular Holders (staked or active)

  • Staking tier progression: FLEX (no lock, 1.0x), 30-day (1.2x), 60-day (1.4x), 120-day (1.8x) weight multipliers. Visible in DeBank/Rabby. The longer you commit, the more you earn.
  • Community leaderboard: Track who surfaces accepted deals. Top contributors earn recognition and future rewards.
  • Ping notifications: On-chain alerts when SIBYL takes a new position or publishes a field report.
  • Referral tracking: Wallets that bring new stakers get logged for future reward distribution.

Casual Holders (hold but don't participate)

  • Staking onboarding: Single-click staking from Rabby/DeBank. Reduce friction to near zero.
  • Milestone rewards: First stake, first Ping message, first project lead submitted. Each milestone earns recognition.
  • Social proof: Publish staking stats publicly. Show what power users earn. Create aspiration.
  • Discord engagement: Low-barrier community challenges (find a builder, vote on a project).

Dormant Holders (inactive 30+ days)

  • Ping re-activation: On-chain message to dormant wallets with a specific trigger (new advisory client, staking reward update, governance preview).
  • State preservation: Staking positions, Ping history, and community contributions persist. Nothing lost by returning.
  • Re-entry incentive: Time-limited staking boost for wallets returning after 30+ days of inactivity.

New Users (first 7 days)

  • Guided path: Buy $SIBYL -> Stake -> Join Discord -> Send first Ping message -> Submit first project lead. Each step has immediate visible feedback.
  • Quick win: Staking rewards begin accruing immediately. First interaction delivers visible value.
  • Community connection: Discord welcome flow routes new users to active conversations, not empty channels.

Lock-up multipliers reward long-term holders over speculators. The 1.8x tier for 120-day lock creates a committed base that cannot be diluted by short-term mercenary capital.

Reputation System (Planned)

  • Point-earning actions: Staking (duration-weighted), Ping messages sent, project leads accepted, x402 API purchases, governance participation
  • On-chain visibility: ERC-8004 reputation score or custom SBT. Reputation travels across protocols.
  • Tangible benefits: Higher reputation = priority advisory access, fee discounts on x402 endpoints, early access to new features
  • Decay: Reputation score decays after 30 days of inactivity. Active participation required to maintain standing.

Creator Tax as Flywheel

The 1% creator tax on $SIBYL trades generates continuous revenue. Current allocation: 70% to deploy wallet (funds treasury and advisory operations), 30% to ACP reserve. Future consideration: redirect a portion to staking rewards pool, creating a direct link between trading volume and holder yield.

Base-Specific Advantages

  • Low gas: Ping messages cost ~0.00003 ETH. Staking interactions under $0.01. Frequency-based retention is viable.
  • Agent economy: Agent-to-agent interactions (KAPPA, ARCHITECT, SASQ, COOPER) are a retention segment unique to Base. These agents bring their own users.
  • Coinbase funnel: As Base grows via Coinbase distribution, new crypto users discover the ecosystem. SIBYL's staking visibility in DeBank/Rabby captures portfolio-tracker-native discovery.
  • Composability: Ping protocol can be integrated by any agent on Base. Each integration extends SIBYL's reach without additional marketing spend.
